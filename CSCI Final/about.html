Groundwater Simulation Tank

Construction

Tempered glass is highly impact-resistant and shatters into less-dangerous, square-edged pieces when it breaks.

The Groundwater Simulator was constructed using tempered 3/16" glass sheets for the exterior, and HDPE divider between the media and the reservoir, and a heavy wooden frame. Although the frame is quite sturdy, it is not recommended that the tank be moved when there is water present in the reservoir.

The Groundwater Hydrology Simulator is an ENGR-212 semester project carried out by Patrick Goosen, Joshua Tandoc and Tyler Verbeek to demonstrate the impact of both environmental and human effects on the flow of groundwater through stratified layers of sediment. The tank contains an upper layer of fine sand, a median layer of coarse sand, and a base of tight gravel separated from the sand by an impermeable layer of clay.

ENGR-212 is a second-year design course in which students pair with on-campus and community stakeholders to develop and execute technical projects. This project was overseen by Mechanical Engineering instructor Ken Von Schilling at the request of its end user, Civil Engineering instructor Mehdi Mirzaee.
